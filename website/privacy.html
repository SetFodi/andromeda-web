Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026 · Contact: pixelweb2026@gmail.com

Andromeda is a local-first desktop browser. Most data never leaves your Mac.

TopicPractice
AccountsNone required
TelemetryNone built into the App
History / bookmarks / SpacesStored on your device
PasswordsEncrypted on device (macOS secure storage)
Ad blocking listsFilter rules cached locally — not a log of sites you visit
Crash logsWritten locally; not uploaded automatically

1. Data stored on your Mac

Depending on use, Andromeda may store Spaces and tabs, history, bookmarks, settings, download metadata, site permission grants, encrypted passwords, adblock filter cache, and local error logs. You can clear browsing data, delete passwords, export backups, or uninstall the App.

2. What we do not collect by default

Andromeda does not require sign-in, sell your data, run its own ad network, upload browsing history, or automatically send crash dumps.

3. Network activity you initiate

When you browse, your device contacts third-party sites. Those parties have their own policies. Shield reduces some third-party requests using public filter lists that may refresh over the network.

4. Update checks

If the App checks for updates (e.g. GitHub Releases), the host may see your IP and that you use Andromeda. That request does not include browsing history.

5. Imports

Imports from Chrome or other browsers are read locally on your machine and stored in Andromeda’s local storage. We do not receive a copy.

6. Children

Andromeda is a general-purpose browser and is not directed at children under 13.

7. Changes

We may update this policy by publishing a new version with a revised date.

8. Contact

Privacy questions: pixelweb2026@gmail.com
