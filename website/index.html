01 Spaces

Every world gets its own weather.

Work, play, and the side project each live in a separate space with their own tabs and their own color — and the entire shell re-tints to match, down to the last hairline. Swipe sideways on the sidebar to move between worlds.

02 Shield

Ads are refused at the door.

Most blockers hide ads after the page has already fetched them. Andromeda declines the request itself — EasyList and EasyPrivacy at the network layer — so pages arrive lighter, faster, and quieter. The shield shows the per-page count; Settings has the off switch.

03 Reader

Every article, set like a book.

One key strips the chrome from any page and re-sets the words in a quiet serif column — the same parsing engine Firefox trusts, wearing far better type. Links survive. Clutter doesn't.

reader mode · ⌘⇧R On the quiet pleasures of a smaller web

Somewhere between the fourth autoplay video and the ninth cookie banner, the page you wanted disappeared. Reader mode is the long walk back — just the words, the pictures that matter, and margins wide enough to think in.

It turns out most articles are lovely once you can see them.

First launch on an unsigned build

Andromeda isn't signed with an Apple Developer certificate yet, so macOS asks once before the first open. On macOS 15+: open the app, dismiss the warning, then System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway. On macOS 14 and older: right-click the app → Open. After that it opens like any other app.
