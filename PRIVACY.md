# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 10 July 2026  
**Product:** Andromeda  
**Contact:** pixelweb2026@gmail.com

This policy describes how Andromeda handles information. It is written for a **local-first** desktop browser: most data never leaves your Mac.

## Summary

| Topic | Practice |
|--------|----------|
| Accounts | None required |
| Telemetry | None built into the App |
| Browsing history / bookmarks / Spaces | Stored **on your device** |
| Passwords | Encrypted on device (macOS `safeStorage` / Keychain-backed) |
| Ad blocking lists | Downloaded to cache filter rules; not your browsing log |
| Crash logs | Written **locally** for debugging; not uploaded automatically |

## 1. Data stored on your Mac

Depending on how you use Andromeda, the App may store on your computer:

- **Spaces and tabs** (session restore)  
- **History, bookmarks, quick links**  
- **Settings and theme preferences**  
- **Download list metadata** (paths and status)  
- **Site permission grants** (camera, mic, location, etc. you allow)  
- **Saved passwords** (encrypted via OS secure storage when available)  
- **Adblock filter cache** (blocklists, not a log of sites you visited)  
- **Local error logs** (for diagnosing crashes)

You can clear browsing data, delete passwords, export/import backups, or uninstall the App to remove local data (subject to normal OS file retention).

## 2. Data we do **not** collect by default

Andromeda does **not**:

- Require sign-in  
- Sell your data  
- Show its own advertising network  
- Phone home with browsing history or keystrokes  
- Upload crash dumps automatically  

## 3. Network activity you initiate

When **you** browse the web, your device contacts third-party sites and services (search engines, websites, CDNs). Those parties have their own privacy policies. We do not control them.

Shield (ad/tracker blocking) reduces some third-party requests by blocking them **on your device** using publicly available filter lists. Lists may be refreshed over the network so blocking stays current.

## 4. Optional update checks

If the App checks for new versions (e.g. GitHub Releases), that request may reveal your IP address and that you use Andromeda to the host serving the release metadata. It does not include your browsing history.

## 5. Imports

If you import bookmarks, history, or passwords from another browser (e.g. Chrome), that data is read **locally** from your machine and stored in Andromeda’s local storage. We do not receive a copy.

## 6. Children

Andromeda is a general-purpose browser and is not directed at children under 13. Do not use the App to knowingly collect personal information from children in violation of applicable law.

## 7. Changes

We may update this policy by publishing a new version with a revised date. Material changes will be reflected in the repository and/or website.
